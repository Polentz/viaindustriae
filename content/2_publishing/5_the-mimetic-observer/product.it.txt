Description: <p>On the occasion of the 700 years since the death of Dante Alighieri, the Central Institute for the Catalog (ICCD) and Documentation proposes the project The Mimetic Observer, the result of a profound analysis and reflection that looks at the world of the Poet in a completely new way: focuses on the objects, landscapes and other natural elements that Dante Alighieri deals with in his major work. The exhibition and the book bring together 27 photographic works created by Carlotta Valente, with the collaboration of Joaquin Paredes, on the theme of light and Dante's imagery in the Divine Comedy. </p><p>The luminous and photographic narration comes to life through a meticulous reconstruction of images and optical illusions taken from the three songs, with the help of the first analog photographic processes, alternating silver salt printing on matte paper, cyanotype on glass, and daguerreotype on metal plates. The photographs are organized in three series of nine images which take up Dante's canticles and are in turn presented according to a different and precise intensity of light (Hell/darkness, Purgatory/light, Paradise/glow). The book opens with a text by Giulio Ferroni. </p><p>The project was supported by the National Committee for the celebrations of the 700th anniversary of the death of Dante Alighieri. The photographs of the animals were created thanks to the collaboration of the Civic Museum of Zoology of Rome.</p>
